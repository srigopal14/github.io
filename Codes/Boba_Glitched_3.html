Project Overview: Advanced Glitch & Layering in Photoshop

This final installment of the Glitch series moves beyond pure data bending into complex **layer and filter manipulation** within Adobe Photoshop to achieve an intentional, digital breakdown aesthetic.

Advanced Digital Art / Compositing

**Adobe Photoshop**

Focus:

RGB Separation & Halftone Effect

Artwork

Detailed Process Description: Boba Glitched 3 (Photoshop)

  1. I imported the image into Photoshop and cleared the background to make it **black**.
  2. I copied and pasted that same boba image twice after masking off the first layer.
  3. For the first copy, I went into the layers section and unchecked both the **Green and Red** color channels (leaving only Blue).
  4. On the second copy, I unchecked both the **Green and Blue** color channels (leaving only Red).
  5. I chose each color layer one at a time and super-imposed the images using the **arrow keys** to create the RGB color separation glitch.
  6. I then clicked on the **Filter Gallery** and applied the **Halftone Pattern** from the Sketch folder.
  7. I ensured that the pattern type was on **Line** mode, **Size on 2**, and **Contrast on 5**.
  8. After that, I went into the **Adjustment Layer** and clicked on the **Curves** tool to move the points into a curved shape to play with the filters.
  9. Then I converted the Curves layer into a **Smart Object**.
  10. Finally, I clicked on the **Lasso Tool** and drew rectangles on various sections to copy and paste them, then moved each section one at a time to show the final **glitch effect** and vertical distortion.
